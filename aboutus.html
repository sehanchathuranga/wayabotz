Electronics Society – ESOC

The Electronics Society is a Students’ Organization associated with the Department of Electronics,Faculty of Applied Sciences of Wayamba University of Sri lanka. It was established in 2007 by the students of the 2004/2005 batch of Faculty of Applied Sciences.

The founder President was Mr. Ruchira Jeewananda, and the initial board members include Mr. Upanith S. Liyanaarachchi and Mr. T. T. D. Ruberu while the Senior Treasurer was Dr. Asanka Karunarathna. The Head of Department of that period was Prof. (Mrs.) G. A. K. S. Perera. The Electronics Society has now grown into a leading organization in the University with the support of all the members of the Department. The Society holds the responsibility of enhancing the electronic related knowledge and the skills of the students of the university and as well as the students of the area.



Mission

The mission of the ESOC is to enabling the member’s careers and promoting community island-wide. The ESOC promotes to innovate, develop, integrate, share, and apply the knowledge about electronics and information technologies and sciences for the benefit of humanity and the profession.



Aims

To provide academic and moral support for all Wayamba university Electronic students.

To promote the study and enjoyment of Electronic within Wayamba University, and out with the community.

To provide and promote a link between Wayamba University Electronic Staff and Students.

IEEE Stubent Branch of Wayamba University of Sri Lanka

IEEE (Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers) is a professional association that develops, defines, and reviews electronics and computer science standards. It was founded in 1963 by merging two organizations. It is the world's largest technical professional organization dedicated to advancing technology for the benefit of humanity.



Vision

IEEE will be essential to the global technical community and to technical professionals everywhere, and be universally recognized for the contributions of technology and of technical professionals in improving global conditions.



Mission

IEEE's core purpose is to foster technological innovation and excellence for the benefit of humanity.

Regional and technical structure with organizational units based on geography and technical focus. It has divided into 10 geographic regions. Sri Lanka is included in Region 10 (Asia and Pacific). It has more than 400,000 members around the globe. IEEE Sri Lanka Section was formed in 2003. And also, there is an IEEE Women in Engineering (WIE) which is one of the largest international professional organizations dedicated to promoting women engineers and scientists and inspiring girls around the world to follow their academic interests to a career in engineering.

IEEE student branch of Wayamba University of Sri Lanka also organized some interesting projects in the last five years. In collaboration with Black Hammer studios, student branch organized INDIESPIRE ’21 which is a two-day game development workshop to improve game development skills and enthusiasm. And they organized O/L seminar series in 2020 targeting O/L examinations. And also helped other societies by collaborating in some interesting projects.
